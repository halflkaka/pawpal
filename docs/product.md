# Product Context

## What PawPal is

PawPal is a pet-first social app for pet owners. Pets are the main characters — owners are in the background. The app helps pets find friends, lets owners share updates about their animals, and builds a community around the animals people love.

This is not a general lifestyle app that happens to include pets. The experience should feel meaningfully different from Instagram or TikTok. Every feature should serve the pet and the pet-owner relationship, not personal brand or self-expression.

## Who it's for

Pet owners who want to:
- Share moments from their pet's life with people who genuinely care
- Find other pets and owners with similar animals, breeds, or locations
- Build a social world centered around their pet, not themselves

## Core principles

**Pets are the protagonists.**
Profiles lead with the pet. The feed is your pet's world. Connections are formed around animals, not people. When in doubt, ask: is this feature about the pet or the person?

**Warmth over scale.**
The tone is playful, warm, and personal — not viral or performative. PawPal is for people who love their animals, not for building an audience.

**Shared experiences over broadcasting.**
The most valuable connections are between owners of similar pets — same breed, same city, same life stage. Features that surface these connections are more valuable than features that increase raw follower counts.

**Simple and focused.**
The app does a few things well rather than many things adequately. New features should deepen the pet-owner experience, not broaden it into general social territory.

## What makes it distinct

- Discovery is about finding compatible pets, not popular people
- The social graph should eventually be pet-to-pet, not just user-to-user
- Content is about the animal's life — moments, milestones, personality — not the owner's lifestyle
- Engagement (likes, comments) happens between owners, but in the context of their pets

## Current gap

The app today is mostly a generic social app with pets attached. The pet-first vision is not fully expressed yet — profiles lead with the user, discovery is mostly static, and the connection layer is user-to-user only. Future development should gradually close this gap.
